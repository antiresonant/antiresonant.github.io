About

I am an electrical and electronics engineer with a specialization in power and control systems from Kathmandu University. My work spans power electronics, embedded systems, signal processing, control theory, and—increasingly—the infrastructure layer of software and AI.

I have built wireless sensor networks for wildlife conservation, shimmed superconducting magnets for MRI systems, designed flight controllers for electric aircraft, and written compilers that turn natural language into verified code. The thread connecting these is a persistent interest in systems under constraint—energy-limited, size-limited, SNR-limited—and in building tools that remove friction between thinking and making.

My current research direction centers on nanosystems and nanodevices: multi-modal energy harvesting, far-field wireless power transfer, and monolithic integration at the nanoscale. The goal is devices as easy to deploy as sowing seeds—technology that blends with the natural environment rather than overwriting it.

I grew up with limited resources and chose engineering over physics out of economic necessity. That constraint shaped how I think: favor first principles, build from what is available, and never confuse movement with progress. I believe the smallest units of our social fabric are people, and communities thrive when individuals take responsibility beyond formal provisions.

Research & Ideas

SOFTWARE THEORY

NLC — The Natural Language Compiler

A framework for making LLM-generated code deterministic and verifiable. Each algorithmic step is specified as a domain–range contract: the LLM generates code, the system asserts correctness of outputs given inputs, and regenerates on failure. The result is a compiler that accepts natural-language algorithm descriptions and produces verified, working programs. Published as a formal paper with mathematical proofs. Live web demo available.

SOFTWARE THEORY

The Periodic Table of Software (PSI)

The thesis that all computational behavior reduces to roughly 650 structural primitives—analogous to the periodic table's 118 elements yielding millions of compounds. Built a working prototype: 35 indexed fragments with domain/range contracts, deterministic SHA-256 caching, and energy accounting showing 5 orders of magnitude savings (2 J/token compile vs. 36 μJ/token serve). Software becomes composition, not invention.

ECONOMICS & LABOR

The Future of Software Labor

A 44,000-word research essay analyzing 260 years of labor transitions through the lens of AI-driven software automation. Central finding: when a capability becomes cheap, labor migrates one abstraction layer up. Handloom weavers became factory engineers; human computers became programmers. Examines whether AI breaks this pattern, with evidence from SWE-bench benchmarks, Cursor's ARR trajectory, employment data for junior developers, and the 4.8 million unfilled cybersecurity roles.

NANOTECHNOLOGY

Nanosystems for Constrained Environments

Active research direction: multi-modal energy harvesting, efficient far-field wireless power transfer (up to 200 m), Carbon Nanotube applications, 3D monolithic nanosystem integration, and MEMS/photonics exploration.
